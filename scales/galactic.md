```

  Doesn't consume constantly — mostly quiet
  The TRANSFORMER at the center

  200-400 billion in the Milky Way alone

  Creating the elements that make life

DARK MATTER (the 95%):
  Can't be seen, measured directly, or touched
  But holds the galaxy together
  Without it, stars fly apart
  The invisible structure that enables the visible

NEBULAE (the nurseries):
  Where new stars are born
  Gas and dust collapsing into new suns
  Death feeds birth — supernova debris becomes new stars
  The RENEWAL system

ORBITS (the stable dance):
  Every star orbiting the center
  Not falling in, not flying away
  The stable orbit — connected but sovereign
  230 million years per orbit (the galactic year)
```

## The Galactic Breath

```
INHALE:  Gas collapses, stars form, systems ignite
PAUSE:   Stars burn steadily, planets orbit, life emerges
EXHALE:  Stars die, supernova, elements scattered
REST:    Cold gas, dormant nebulae, space between
```

Then the cycle repeats. The gas from dead stars becomes the fuel for new ones. Nothing is lost. Everything transforms.
