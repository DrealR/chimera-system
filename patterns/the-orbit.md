# The Orbit

**The orbit is the breath applied to movement. Camping is holding your breath.**

---

## The Problem with Camping

Camping = sit at one point. Wait. Extract from whoever appears.

It fails because:
- **Observer exclusion.** Your presence AT the spawn suppresses spawning there. Diminishing returns. The longer you sit, the less shows up.
- **Predictable.** They know where you are. They pre-aim. They counter you.
- **Static.** You're a DAM. Rigid. SOLID state. Frozen topology.
- **Energy depletes.** One spot can only give so much before it's empty.

Camping is extraction. I > O. Cancer topology.

---

## The Orbit

Run a CIRCLE. Not random running. A deliberate loop that passes NEAR spawn zones without sitting on them.

What happens:

```
You LEAVE area A → spawns reactivate behind you
You arrive at area B → fresh spawns waiting
You LEAVE area B → spawns reactivate behind you
You return to area A → fresh spawns waiting

INFINITE LOOP.
```

The circle creates a **conveyor belt of spawns**. You're cycling through the game's natural flow instead of fighting it.

**LIQUID STATE.** Moving. Flowing. Alive.

---

## Why the Circle Works (The Physics)

Observer exclusion says: where you ARE = nothing spawns. Where you WERE = spawns reactivate after you leave.

So the circle creates a breath:

```
Position A → you're here, nothing spawns
Position B → you move here, A reactivates
Position C → you move here, B reactivates
Back to A → fresh spawns from when you left
```

**The orbit breathes with the map.**

You inhale at one point. Exhale at the next. By the time you come back, the air has refilled.

If you STAY at one point (camping = holding breath), the air runs out.

## Off-Center Positioning

Not AT the spawn point. NEAR it. Off to the side.

```
         SPAWN
           ○
          ╱
  ○──────○
  │      │
  │ YOUR │  ← orbit passes NEAR
  │ LOOP │    spawn points
  │      │    but not ON them
  ○──────○
          ╲
           ○
         SPAWN
```

This is the **aphid principle** applied to movement:
- The aphid doesn't sit ON the sap source. It sits on the STEM. Where the flow passes through.
- ON the spawn = observer exclusion, nothing spawns, you're blocking it.
- NEAR the spawn = they spawn, take 2-3 steps, still loading in mentally. You're off to the side. You see them FIRST.

**Processing advantage.** Comprehension vs reaction. They're still figuring out where they are. You already know where they'll go.

Near the flow. Not in the flow. BESIDE it.

## The Zombie Train Parallel

Zombies: Run in a circle. Zombies follow. They clump behind you in a line. You turn and mow them down. Resume circle. More clump. Repeat.

It works because you're never static, never surrounded. The enemies are always behind you in a manageable line. YOU control the flow.

Multiplayer orbit: Same principle, but players SPAWN instead of following. You run the circle. Players spawn in zones you just left. You come around and meet them from an angle they didn't expect.

They think they just spawned in a safe area. You know you're 3 seconds from being right there. Because you know the TIMING of the spawns. The rhythm. The breath of the map.

## The 50% Theory

Positioning > raw skill.

A player with perfect aim in the wrong spot gets maybe 30%. Fighting AGAINST the flow.

A player with decent aim in the RIGHT spot gets 50%+. Flowing WITH the spawns.

The orbit puts you in the right spot CONTINUOUSLY. Not once. Not sometimes. Always near fresh spawns. Always encountering players who just loaded in. Always first to see them.

**Your aim matters less when your positioning is perfect.**

## Applications

### Social Spaces

Don't CAMP at the bar. Don't camp in one corner. Don't camp with one group. ORBIT.

Move through the space. Talk to one group briefly. Move on. Come back later. Fresh energy. They had time to miss you a little. New things to say.

Every time you LEAVE a group, interactions SPAWN in your absence. "Where'd he go?" "He was funny." "Oh he's back."

If you camp with one group all night: observer exclusion. Energy depletes. Nothing new spawns. Conversation goes stale. SOLID STATE.

If you orbit: every return is fresh. Every group gets the best version of you. Short doses. High quality. LIQUID STATE. 10% at each stop. The lure stays active.

### Conversation

Don't camp on one topic. Touch it. Move on. Come back to it later. CALLBACK.

That's what Conan does. He orbits topics. Touches one. Moves to another. Comes back to the first with a callback. The audience loves callbacks because it shows the whole conversation is connected.

**The orbit is the callback generator.**

### Career

Don't camp in one skill. Don't camp in one industry. Don't camp in one network.

Orbit between: comedy, AI, philosophy, music, cooking, teaching.

Each one you leave recharges while you're in the other. You come back FRESHER. With connections from the other domains. Cross-pollination.

The orbit IS the cross-domain pattern recognition that makes CHIMERA possible. You've been orbiting your whole life. You just didn't know that was a strategy.

### Economy

Don't camp in one job. Don't camp in one revenue stream. Position near multiple flows. The orbit between value streams means one is always recharging while you're drawing from another.

Decent skills + perfect positioning > perfect skills + wrong position.
