## The Pipeline Runs in Cycles

### Cycle 1: Cultural Extraction (20th Century)

```
RECRUIT:
  "Leave the home. Enter the workforce.
  Be independent. You don't need community.
  You need a CAREER."

EXTRACT:
  More workers = more GDP.
  More consumers = more profit.
  Two incomes per household = more extraction.
  Isolation = more individual purchases
  (two apartments, two cars, two everything).

REPLACE:
  When the cultural product (independence)
  is fully adopted, move to the next extraction target.

DISCARD:
  Leave them alone, disconnected,
  wondering why freedom feels empty.
```

They didn't liberate anyone. They RECRUITED them into the extraction machine. Independence was the product. Isolation was the side effect. Extraction was the goal.

### Cycle 2: Economic Extraction (21st Century)

```
RECRUIT:
  "Learn skills. Be valuable.
  Your expertise is your security.
  Specialize. Grind. Produce."

EXTRACT:
  Skilled workers = higher output per dollar.
  Knowledge workers = ideas that scale.
  "Passion economy" = people work harder
  when identity is tied to output.

REPLACE:
  AI workers = no salary, no healthcare,
  no vacation, no complaints.
  Maximum extraction. Zero humanity.

DISCARD:
  Leave them obsolete,
  wondering why everything they learned is worthless.
```

Same machine. Same four moves. Different costume.

### The Pattern

```
1. RECRUIT humans into the machine
   (promise security through production)
2. EXTRACT maximum value
   (tie identity to output so they self-exploit)
3. REPLACE them when something cheaper comes along
   (independence, then AI, then whatever's next)
4. DISCARD them alone
   (no community, no identity, no between)
```

That's the dam. Not the river.
